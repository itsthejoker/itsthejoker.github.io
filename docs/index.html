Projects I've done or contributed to!
Grafeas Group, Ltd. - grafeas.org

A small international nonprofit that I co-founded with James Coe, this registered 501(c)3 in the US has over 5,750 volunteers and aims to increase accessibility on the internet. By creating text versions of internet content, everyone has a chance to participate in the discussion. Our volunteers have, at the time of this writing, completed over 260,000 transcriptions and descriptions online and through Reddit for those who need them.

It is entirely donation funded and is the sole sponsor for our subreddit / primary operations at TranscribersOfReddit.

Yarn Calc-Inator - itsthejoker.github.io/yarncalcinator/

A small site that helps perform basic conversions for yarn -- helpful for interpreting crochet and knitting patterns across meters and yards. Doesn't go into the more complex side of dealing with yarn density, but is enough to generally answer the question of "okay wait seriously how many skeins do I really need for thisâ€½".

TranscribersOfReddit - reddit.com/r/TranscribersOfReddit

A mostly automated subreddit, this exercise in gamification, volunteerism, and accessibility was started in April 2017. Since then, we have gained volunteers on every continent in the world (including Antarctica!) and have become an important part of the accessibility-related landscape of Reddit.com.

FilamentColors.xyz - FilamentColors.xyz

Lots of problems need to be solved, but many problems only get solved when someone is annoyed enough. In this case, the 3D printing hobby has a well-known problem: the plastic filaments that you buy online might not be the same color that you see online.

To address that, I built FilamentColors.xyz, a Django / Bootstrap app that indexes 3D printed swatches by their color and uses basic color theory to match plastics from different manufacturers together in order to help people plan for projects. At current, the site receives just under 1k visitors per month.
